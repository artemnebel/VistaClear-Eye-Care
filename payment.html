Vision Coverage

Vision Insurance

We work directly with vision insurers to submit claims on your behalf. Our experienced staff is trained to handle insurance coverage questions and help you maximize your benefits.

Many current vision plans are designed to cover most basic vision-related costs, but not all costs associated with eye care. Most plans require co-payments for office visits plus additional out-of-pocket costs for frames, lenses, or specialty services.

To verify that we accept your specific plan, please call us at 215-935-6908 before your visit. Steve Park, our Assistant Manager, specializes in insurance and billing and can answer any questions about your coverage.

What Vision Insurance Typically Covers

Annual Eye Examination

Most plans cover one comprehensive eye exam per year with a standard co-pay.

Prescription Eyeglasses

Frame and lens allowances vary by plan. Premium lenses (progressives, anti-reflective coatings) often require an upgrade cost.

Contact Lens Allowance

Most plans include an annual contact lens allowance that can be applied toward your lenses and fitting fee.

Medical Eye Conditions

Treatment for glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy, or other medical conditions may be billed to medical insurance rather than vision insurance.

Tax-Advantaged Funds

FSA & HSA Accounts

Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA) and Health Savings Accounts (HSA) allow you to set aside pre-tax dollars for medical and eye care expenses — reducing what you actually pay out of pocket.

FSA and HSA funds can be used alongside your vision insurance to cover co-pays, deductibles, frames, contact lenses, and even LASIK surgery. This is one of the smartest ways to reduce your eye care costs.

Learn More at Your Visit

FSA Eligible Eye Care Expenses

  • Comprehensive eye exams & co-pays
  • Prescription eyeglasses & frames
  • Contact lenses & contact lens solutions
  • LASIK eye surgery
  • Prescription sunglasses
  • Reading glasses (if prescribed)
Year-End FSA Reminder

Most FSA accounts have a "use it or lose it" policy. Use remaining balances before year-end by scheduling an appointment or ordering new glasses or contacts.

Common Questions

Insurance FAQ

Do you accept my insurance?

We accept most major vision insurance plans. The best way to confirm coverage is to call our office at 215-935-6908 before your appointment. Our staff will verify your benefits and let you know what to expect in terms of co-pays and coverage.

Do you accept American Express?

We accept all major credit cards with the exception of American Express. Visa, Mastercard, and Discover are all accepted.

Can I use my FSA for contact lenses?

Yes. Contact lenses, contact lens solutions, prescription eyeglasses, and eye exams are all FSA-eligible expenses. LASIK surgery is also an eligible FSA expense.

What if I don't have vision insurance?

No problem. We accept cash, checks, and major credit cards. We're also happy to discuss payment arrangements and financing options before your appointment so there are no surprises.

Does vision insurance cover LASIK?

Most standard vision insurance plans do not cover LASIK surgery, though some plans offer discounts. LASIK is, however, an FSA/HSA-eligible expense. We can discuss financing options to help make the procedure accessible.

How do I know what my vision plan covers?

Call the member services number on the back of your insurance card, or bring your insurance card to your appointment and let us look up your benefits. Our team is experienced at decoding insurance plans.

Have Billing Questions?

Our team is here to help you understand your insurance benefits and payment options. Don't let cost concerns prevent you from getting the eye care you need.
