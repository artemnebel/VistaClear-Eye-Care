Preventative Care

Eye & Vision Exams

Preventative and routine eye exams are critical to maintaining good eye health. Many vision problems develop without obvious symptoms and can only be diagnosed by a licensed optometrist. Early detection enables treatment that prevents vision loss.

The American Optometric Association recommends annual or biennial exams depending on your age and risk factors. Dr. Lee conducts a comprehensive consultation covering your symptoms, medications, work environment, health history, and family eye conditions.

Vision Correction

Contact Lens Exams

Dr. Lee conducts a thorough eye and vision assessment, then discusses contact lens options best suited to your vision needs and lifestyle. The practice addresses concerns like dry eye, allergies, and recurring infections when determining lens suitability.

After determining refractive error using a phoropter or automated instrument, Dr. Lee collaborates with you to decide between contacts and glasses. Contact lenses are available in soft or rigid gas-permeable form with varying replacement schedules — daily, weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly.

Advanced cosmetic lenses can also camouflage color variations or transform your natural eye color.

Surgical Co-Management

LASIK Eye Surgery

LASIK (laser assisted in situ keratomilesis) is a corrective alternative to glasses or contact lenses. Dr. Lee provides comprehensive pre- and post-operative care and referrals to an established LASIK surgery center for advanced diagnostic testing and procedure options.

LASIK effectively treats myopia (nearsightedness), hyperopia (farsightedness), and astigmatism. If you're deemed a candidate, you'll receive a referral to the affiliated center for the procedure itself.

LASIK Consultation

Pre-Surgical Evaluation

Comprehensive patient history review, eye health assessment, and candidacy determination. Confirms prescription stability and overall health for surgery.

Referral & Procedure

Referral to affiliated LASIK center for the procedure. Advanced technology delivers fast recovery and precise results.

Post-Operative Care

Follow-ups begin the day after surgery and continue at prearranged intervals over six months. Annual exams assess long-term results.

Diagnostic Imaging

Retinal Photography

Retinal photography is an excellent method for Dr. Lee to get a detailed picture of your retina — either to diagnose ocular disease or to have as a baseline reading should any abnormality arise in the future.

This non-invasive imaging technique captures high-resolution images of the retina, optic disk, and macula, enabling accurate monitoring over time and early detection of conditions like diabetic retinopathy, macular degeneration, and glaucoma.

Disease Detection

Glaucoma Testing

Glaucoma is one of the leading causes of blindness, yet often has no early symptoms. Our practice uses the Humphrey Field Analyzer for visual field testing — the gold standard in detecting glaucoma suspects and monitoring patients taking vision-affecting medications.

Tonometry during your eye exam measures intraocular pressure, a key indicator of glaucoma risk. Early detection through routine screening is the most effective way to preserve your vision.

Digital Eye Health

Computer Vision Care

Screens are everywhere — and digital eye strain is a growing concern. Computer vision syndrome affects millions who spend extended time on computers, phones, and tablets. Symptoms include headaches, blurred vision, dry eyes, and neck pain.

Dr. Lee evaluates and treats computer-related vision issues and recommends tailored solutions — from specialized lenses and screen filters to the 20-20-20 rule and ergonomic adjustments.

Get Relief Today

Common Symptoms

  • Eye strain and fatigue
  • Blurred or double vision
  • Headaches and neck pain
  • Dry, red, or irritated eyes
  • Difficulty focusing after screen time

The 20-20-20 Rule

Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for at least 20 seconds. This simple habit dramatically reduces digital eye strain for screen users.

Frame Guide by Face Shape

Square Face

Strong jaw, broad forehead. Choose curved frames with more horizontal than vertical elements to soften and elongate.

Oval Face

Balanced proportions. Wide frames that follow the brow line work best — diamond or rectangular shapes complement naturally.

Round Face

Angular, narrow frames wider than they are deep add length and balance. Avoid round styles that exaggerate facial curves.

Heart & Triangle Face

Frames wider at the bottom balance a broad forehead. Cat-eye, aviator, and light-colored or rimless styles work well.

Diamond Face

Soft oval frames or rimless and cat-eye styles highlight cheekbones and complement the distinctive shape beautifully.
